Gaming PC vs Office PC

The General Components

Component Gaming PC Office PC
Motherboard High-End Motherboard with many bells and whistles such as over-clocking capabilities. Simple, Low-End Motherboard with barebones functions.
CPU Starting from High-End Dual Core to Extreme Enthusiast Octa-Core CPUs with overclocking capabilities. (Intel Core i5 through to i7 Extreme) Low-End Dual Core CPU, powerful enough to do basic tasks but nothing intensive. (Intel Core i3)
GPU High-End gaming oriented GPUs, more than one in many High-End Gaming Setups for Next-Generation performance. CPU Integrated Graphics or a very Low-End graphics card.
Storage SSD for the main OS and HDDs for Games. HDD
RAM Large amount of high speed, low latency RAM, anywhere from 8GB to 32GB or even 64GB. Average Speed, Low-End RAM, about 4GB to 8GB.
PSU 700W through to 1500W PSUs. 300W throught to 700W.
Cost Starting from around $700 for Low-End gaming PCs to $1500 for Mid-Range, and $2500-3000 and above for High-End gaming PCs. Starting from around $650 through to $1000.

The Comparison

In both the Gaming PC and the Office PC start at around the same starting price. This is likely due to the similar components in each, as basic computers share similar parts.

The biggest differences between the Gaming PC and the Office PC is the horse power and the cost of the systems. The Gaming PC sports powerful graphics card(s), more powerful CPUs, more RAM, and faster storage compared to the Office PC. This is because gaming is a much more power hungry and intesive task for the computer. Office computers only have to put up with basic word processing, web browsing and other basic tasks that do not require much power.
Another key difference between Gaming PCs and Office PCs is the cooling. As Gaming PCs do more intesive, power consuming tasks, it outputs alot of heat. Due to this, Gaming PCs must be cooled far better than Office PCs that do barely any work at all.

One of the most important components in a Gaming PC is the Graphics Card (GPU). This is because the graphics card does all of the rendering of the frames in the game which you percieve as motion on the screen. If the graphics card is not powerful enough for the game you want to play, the game may not be playable at all or run very slowly so that the percieved motion is very stuttery.
Other important components in a Gaming PC are fast storage, fast RAM with low latency, and a relativly powerful CPU. All of these components do not have as much of an affect on the gameplay compared to the graphics card, but may act as bottlenecks for the overall performance of the computer.
